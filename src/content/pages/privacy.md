This is the privacy notice for Sevenoaks Saxes. It explains what personal data we collect, and how and why we use it.

We review this privacy notice regularly. It was last reviewed on 16 February 2026.

## How and why we use your information

### Members

If you are a member of the group, we collect the following information:

- name and contact details
- payment history
- your availability for rehearsals and concerts
- the instruments you play
- photos and video footage

We use this information to:

- administer your membership
- let you know about our activities and events, including rehearsals and concerts
- check if and when you’ve paid your subscription fees and follow up if you’ve missed a payment
- organise deputies for rehearsals and concerts
- reduce scheduling clashes when organising our activities and events
- publicise the group using photos and video footage

We collect this information directly from you verbally or by email.

We only retain this information as long as you are a member of the group or for as long as is necessary to perform the tasks above, whichever is shortest.

Our lawful basis for collecting and using this information is for the **performance of a contract**. In this case, your membership of the group.

We also rely on your **consent** to use your contact details to send you emails and use photo and video footage. You may withdraw your consent at any time.

### Workers

If you work for the group, we collect the following information:

- name and contact details
- right to work
- details of any criminal convictions
- details of any Disclosure and Barring Service (DBS) checks
- payment details

We use this information to:

- administer the contract that we have with you
- ensure you have a right to work in the UK
- pay you

Our lawful bases for collecting and using this information are to **comply with a contract** and to **comply with legal requirements**.

## Your rights

You have the right to request:

- information about how your personal data is processed
- a copy of that personal data
- that anything inaccurate in your personal data is corrected without undue delay

You can also:

- raise an objection about how your personal data is processed
- request that your personal data is erased if there is no longer a justification for it
- ask that the processing of your personal data is restricted in certain circumstances

If you wish to exercise your rights above, please contact us.

## How to complain

If you have any concerns about our use of your personal data, you can make a complaint to us.

If you remain unhappy with how we’ve used your data after raising a complaint with us, you can also complain to the Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO).

The ICO’s address is: Information Commissioner’s Office, Wycliffe House, Water Lane, Wilmslow, Cheshire, SK9 5AF

You can also contact the ICO by phone on 0303 123 1113 or on their [website](https://www.ico.org.uk/make-a-complaint).
